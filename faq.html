Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about bookkeeping, taxes, and financial services in Hawaii.

How much does bookkeeping cost in Hawaii? +
Our monthly bookkeeping starts at $199/month for startups and small businesses with limited transactions. Growing businesses with more transactions and complexity typically invest $299–$499/month. We provide a free consultation to assess your needs and provide an accurate quote. Our rates are all-inclusive — no surprise fees.
Do you handle GET filing for my Hawaii business? +
Yes. Hawaii General Excise Tax filing is included in our bookkeeping services. We track your taxable gross receipts, prepare your monthly, quarterly, or semi-annual G-45 returns, file electronically through Hawaii Tax Online, and reconcile with your annual G-49. We also advise on proper GET rate application and passing-on methods to ensure accuracy and compliance.
Can you help if my books are a mess? +
Absolutely — this is actually one of our specialties. Bookkeeping catch-up and cleanup is a common request. We'll go back to the beginning of the year (or further if needed), categorize and reconcile all transactions, identify discrepancies, and bring your books up to current. We provide catch-up bookkeeping pricing based on the number of months needed.
Do you work with all industries or specialize? +
We work with a wide range of Hawaii businesses including restaurants and food service, retail, construction and contracting, professional services (attorneys, consultants, medical practices), tourism and hospitality, real estate, and e-commerce. We have particular depth in GET-heavy businesses and seasonal tourism-related businesses that require specialized financial management.
Do I need to provide paper documents? +
No. We work 100% digitally. We connect to your bank and credit card accounts via secure read-only bank feeds in QuickBooks, and you can share receipts and documents via a secure client portal. We have clients across Oahu and other islands who we've never met in person. Our service is designed to be fully remote and paperless.
How quickly can you start working on my books? +
After signing a service agreement and providing banking access, we can typically begin within 3–5 business days. For urgent situations like an upcoming tax deadline or audit, we offer expedited onboarding. The free consultation is usually scheduled within 1–2 business days of your request.
Are you a CPA firm? +
Hale Bookkeeping is a bookkeeping and tax preparation firm. Our founder Alicia Nakamura holds a CPA license, and we offer tax preparation services for individuals and businesses. We partner with CPA firms for complex tax matters requiring audit or attest services, and we work seamlessly with your existing CPA if you have one.
What software do you use? +
We primarily use QuickBooks Online, which gives you real-time access to your financial data from any device. We are also proficient in QuickBooks Desktop, Xero, and Wave for clients with specific preferences. For tax preparation we use professional tax software that supports all Hawaii and federal forms.
Can I view my books any time? +
Yes. With QuickBooks Online, you have 24/7 access to all your financial data, reports, and records from any device. We set up your account with the appropriate access level so you can check your P&L, outstanding invoices, or bank balances any time you need. Monthly we send a financial review package summarizing key metrics and any notable items to address.
Do you offer payroll services? +
Yes. Full-service payroll is available as a standalone service or bundled with bookkeeping. We handle all aspects of Hawaii payroll including federal and state withholding calculations, TDI, UI, direct deposit setup, pay stub distribution, quarterly 941 filings, and annual W-2 and 1099 preparation. We stay current on all Hawaii payroll regulatory changes.
