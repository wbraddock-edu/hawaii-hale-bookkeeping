How to Register a Business in Hawaii: A Step-by-Step Guide

By Hale Bookkeeping Team · April 10, 2024 · 6 min read

Starting a business in Hawaii involves several registration steps with different state and federal agencies. The process can seem complicated, but with the right guidance it is manageable — and getting it right from the start prevents compliance issues down the road.

Step 1: Choose Your Business Structure

The most common structures for Hawaii small businesses are: Sole Proprietorship (simple, no state registration required, but no liability protection), LLC (provides liability protection with pass-through taxation, most popular for small businesses), Corporation (S-corp or C-corp status, more formal structure with additional compliance requirements), and Partnership (for businesses with multiple owners). Most new Hawaii small businesses benefit from forming an LLC, which balances simplicity with meaningful liability protection.

Step 2: Register with Hawaii DCCA

Corporations, LLCs, partnerships, and businesses using a trade name (DBA) must register with the Hawaii Department of Commerce and Consumer Affairs (DCCA) Business Registration Division. You can register online at Hawaii Business Express or file paper forms by mail. The standard LLC filing fee is $50. Approved filings receive a Certificate of Organization and are listed in the DCCA public database.

Step 3: Obtain a Federal EIN

An Employer Identification Number (EIN) from the IRS is required for any business with employees, any corporation or partnership, and is recommended for all LLCs. You can apply for free at irs.gov/ein and receive your EIN immediately online. The EIN is your business's federal tax identification number used for all federal tax filings, business bank accounts, and payroll tax accounts.

Step 4: Get a Hawaii GET License

Before making your first taxable transaction, you must register for Hawaii's General Excise Tax through Hawaii Tax Online at hitax.hawaii.gov. There is a one-time $20 fee per business location. You will receive your HI tax ID and license certificate, which must be displayed at your business location. GET registration also covers other Hawaii taxes like use tax and employer withholding.

Step 5: Register for Payroll Taxes (If You Have Employees)

Businesses with employees must register for federal payroll taxes, Hawaii state income tax withholding, Hawaii Unemployment Insurance (UI) with the DLIR, and Hawaii TDI (Temporary Disability Insurance). Hawaii requires employers to provide TDI coverage — either through the state plan or an approved private insurance carrier. Payroll compliance in Hawaii has several state-specific requirements that differ from other states, making professional guidance particularly valuable.
