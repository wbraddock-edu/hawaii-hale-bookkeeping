Bookkeeping Basics for New Business Owners

By Hale Bookkeeping Team · May 18, 2024 · 6 min read

Starting a business in Hawaii is exciting — but the financial side can feel overwhelming. Between GET registration, opening a business bank account, tracking expenses, and preparing for tax time, many new owners don't know where to begin. This guide covers the foundational bookkeeping practices every new Hawaii business owner should implement from day one.

Open a Separate Business Bank Account Immediately

This is the most important step. A dedicated business checking account — completely separate from your personal accounts — is the foundation of clean bookkeeping. It creates a clear legal and financial boundary between your personal and business finances, simplifies tax preparation, supports your liability protection as an LLC or corporation, and makes it easy to track all business transactions in one place.

Choose an Accounting Method: Cash vs. Accrual

Cash basis accounting records income when cash is received and expenses when paid. Accrual accounting records income when earned and expenses when incurred, regardless of when money changes hands. Most small Hawaii businesses start with cash basis because it's simpler and more intuitive. However, if you extend credit to customers or carry significant inventory, accrual accounting may give you a more accurate picture of your financial position.

Understand Your Key Financial Statements

Three reports tell you everything about your business finances. The Profit and Loss Statement shows revenues, expenses, and net profit for a period. The Balance Sheet shows what you own (assets), what you owe (liabilities), and the difference (equity) at a point in time. The Cash Flow Statement shows how cash moved in and out of your business. Review these monthly — most bookkeeping software generates them automatically.

Track Every Business Expense

Every business expense is a potential tax deduction. Common deductible expenses for Hawaii businesses include rent, utilities, payroll, GET paid to vendors, professional services, insurance, vehicle expenses, marketing, and supplies. Keep receipts for every expense — cloud-based apps like Dext or Hubdoc can photograph and categorize receipts automatically, saving you hours of work at tax time.

Set Up GET Record Keeping

From the moment you begin collecting GET, you need records that support your filings. Most accounting software can track taxable gross receipts by GET category. Know your filing frequency and set calendar reminders for due dates. Hawaii penalizes late GET filings with steep percentage-based penalties, so consistency from day one is crucial.
